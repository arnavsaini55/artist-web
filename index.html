In a quaint village, the extraordinary Babita Agarwal defied norms and pursued her passion for crafts long before marriage. After tying the knot, her love for crafts flourished, and she saw an opportunity to empower struggling artisans. With boundless determination, she founded “Aasha Mahila Bal Kalyan,” becoming a beacon of hope for over 3000 artists seeking recognition. When COVID struck, Babita adapted, shifting her focus to crafting home clinic items with her skilled team. Surprisingly, her success lay in crafting unique pieces based on demand, rather than flashy packaging. She streamlined operations and embraced a “learning by doing” approach, fostering her artistic growth. Beyond crafting, Babita forged deep emotional connections with customers, making her NGO shine. Her efforts uplifted the entire town, inspiring aspiring entrepreneurs and artists. Babita’s tale continues to weave empowerment and prosperity, one stitch at a time

Meet Ghulam Abbas, a highly skilled and experienced Chikankari artisan whose craft has been a treasured legacy passed down through generations in his family. Drawing inspiration from his father, a national award winner in 1968, Ghulam Abbas has embraced the art of Chikankari with profound passion and dedication. Currently residing in the charming neighborhood of Ghasmandi Dhaal in Lucknow, Ghulam Abbas shares his life with his beloved wife, Sabina, and their two children. His journey into the world of Chikankari began at a tender age of 10, where he was immersed in the rich cultural surroundings that celebrated this exquisite craft. Over the span of more than 45 years, he has honed his skills, becoming a true master in his craft. Ghulam Abbas’s artistic prowess has led him to partake in various notable events, with the Golden Eye Project in 1984-85 being a standout moment. Additionally, he has represented the art of Chikankari on the international stage, participating in fairs and events abroad. The Dubai Shopping Festival in 1996-97, the Demonstration in India High Commission, Abu Dhabi in 1997, and The Great India Festival in Taipei, Taiwan, showcased his exceptional talent to a global audience. An expert in 26 different stitches of Chikankari, Ghulam Abbas has ventured beyond traditional applications, introducing innovative ideas that have earned him immense acclaim. One of his most unique creations was adorning holi caps with his craft, featuring a special motif of an elephant playing holi. The caps became an instant hit among the Marathi people, as the designs of elephants and mangoes hold special significance in their culture. With deep wisdom gained over years of experience, Ghulam Abbas emphasizes that mastering the art of Chikankari requires at least two decades of dedicated practice. His expertise has been acknowledged and appreciated by various educational institutions, and he has been associated with prestigious institutions like NIFT Mumbai

Meet Tarannum Tauheed, a talented artisan who has transformed her passion for Chikankari into a thriving entrepreneurial venture. With three years of dedicated business experience, she follows in the footsteps of her mother and sisters, hailing from the renowned Kakori village, known for its skilled practitioners of this craft. Driven by a desire to forge her own path, Tarannum embarked on this artistic journey, and though she feels she has achieved much, she envisions reaching even greater heights in the future. With over 25 years of hands-on expertise in Chikankari, she possesses a deep understanding of the craft, which proved instrumental in establishing her own brand. From the intricacies of embroidery to the meticulous details of packaging, Tarannum and her team handle every aspect of their business. Her dedication and skill have brought immense success, with orders pouring in from overseas clients in remarkably little time. Maintaining a simple website managed by her son, she efficiently handles customer inquiries and orders through this platform. Tarannum’s commitment to her craft is evident in every facet of her work, including the careful curation of her unique wooden blocks, featuring her exclusive designs. Primarily catering to wholesale retailers, she also owns a charming boutique where her craftsmanship takes center stage. Quality is of utmost importance to Tarannum, and she goes to great lengths to ensure the proper storage of her exquisite creations. With a keen eye for detail, she keeps her products in immaculate conditions, employing red chillies and golis to protect them from any unwanted insects. Tarannum’s artistic prowess has earned her collaborations with renowned personalities such as designer Anil Bhandari and the esteemed singer Kanika Kapoor. Her journey as an artisan turned entrepreneur is a testament to the power of passion, dedication, and innovation, and she continues to make a mark in the world of Chikankari with her artistic  brilliance

Meet Rana Ahmed, an exceptionally skilled artisan hailing from the vibrant streets of Fatehganj Galla Mandi in Lucknow. With an impressive experience of over 35 years in her craft, she is truly a master of her art. Notably, Rana Ahmed holds a degree in Home Science, reflecting her dedication to her passion and pursuit of excellence. Rana draws her inspiration from a remarkable source - her mother, who was a gold medallist in her own right. Growing up in the beautiful city of Kanpur, Rana shared her childhood with four sisters and two brothers, where creativity and craftsmanship were nurtured from an early age. At the tender age of 10, Rana embarked on her journey into the world of artistry, guided by a friend of her sister. From that moment, her skills began to blossom, and there was no looking back. Even after her marriage in 1993, Rana continued to pursue her craft with unwavering determination, proving that her passion knew no bounds. Determined to continually improve her craft, she is always seeking ways to enhance her skills. Her dedication is evident in every creation she brings to life. Not content with merely perfecting her art, she also takes the initiative to make her own cotton thread, showcasing her commitment to producing high-quality work. Beyond her individual achievements, Rana is passionate about empowering others through her craft. As a testament to her benevolence, she runs training centres not only within the bustling city of Lucknow but also in the surrounding villages. Through these initiatives, she imparts her knowledge and expertise, shaping the artisans of tomorrow and preserving the rich heritage of Chikankari. Rana Ahmed’s journey is a testament to the power of determination and the love for one’s craft. With every stitch she weaves, she adds to the timeless legacy of Chikankari, leaving an indelible mark on the world of traditional Indian  craftsmanship

AWADH - E - KAARI

Chikankari is a traditional embroidery style that originated in the city of Lucknow, India and flourished under the patronage of the rulers of Awadh. The Nawabs of Awadh manufactured the exquisite textiles in Lucknow. Later when the capital of Awadh shifted to Lucknow from Faizabad, in the year 1722, the knowledge of the craft came to Lucknow. It is renowned for its delicate and intricate white threadwork on fine muslin fabric, creating beautiful patterns and designs. The word “Chikan” steps from a Persian word derived from “Chic”, which referred to the “Jali” work done on marble or wood. It is also famous as “shadow work” or “white embroidery work”. Some claim that Chikan appears to have been derived from the Persian word “chikin” or “chakin”, which means making delicate pattern over fabrics or cloth wrought with needlework. According to Laila Tyabji, Chikankari stems from the white-on-white embroidery of Shiraz came to India as part of a culture of Persian nobles at the Mughal court. There were chikankaars , artisans who practiced in the courts of Kolkata, Delhi, Dhaka (Bangladesh), Gaya, Varanasi, Allahabad, Rampur and Bhopal. In Lucknow, the Nawabs of Awadh made the finely embroidered muslins a prescribed requirement of the ceremonial court.
